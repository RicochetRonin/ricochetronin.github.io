Postmortem

Thing that went right:

Overall, it seems like I was able to slot into this team, and fill in places that needed to be filled.
I think what I was able to contribute in terms of art assets, narrative, and level design along with some team organization was helpful.
I learned a lot about what game development on a team of people looks like, and the amount of communication and teamwork required to make each piece work.

Things that went wrong:

I wish I was able to design more advanced levels, but we didn't really know what assets we'd need until we needed them, leading to some missing things.
Overall we had a lack of artists and professionalism in a lot of our presentations. While personally I don't find this to be too bad of a thing, it definitely made our project look a little lackluster compared to the other team.
Some weeks it felt like a bit of scramble to get everything in that we needed, a little more organization there would have been good and I should have stepped up more for that.

Lessons learned:

Overall, game development even on this small scale with fairly small teams, requires a lot of planning, organization, and teamwork.
It is essential to sit down with teammates and develop/work together in order to ensure that everyone's proper vision for the game is implemented.
Pixel art, and developing a pixel art world, is far harder than it seems, but with the right amount of time dedicated, we can make it work.

Next Project:

For my next project, I want to step up more when I'm needed in terms of organization and management. I want to make sure we are getting things done that need to be done, so we don't have to scramble.
Also, I would like to do some programming and development of my next project, but given how many programmers we had already, other shoes needed to filled this time.
